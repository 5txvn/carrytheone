## General Explanation
To derive a trick, we can use some simple algebra. Upon expanding the expression, we get $a^2+(2a)^2=a^2+4a^2=5a^2$. Thus, we can find $a^2+(2a)^2$ by simply squaring a, dividing it by 2, and then multiplying it by 10 ($5a^2=\frac{a^2}2\times 10$).

2. Divide it by 2 to get 32 ($64\div 2=32$)
3. Multiply this result by 10 to get 320 ($32\times 10=320$)
4. Final Answer: **320**
