# Sum of Triangular Numbers
## Notes
None.
## General Explanation
Use the formula $\frac{n(n+1)(n+2)}6$, where n is the nth triangular number you're summing.
## Example 1
$$1+3+6+\dots+28=$$
1. 28 is the 7th triangular number, so n=7 and the answer is $\frac{7(7+1)(7+2)}6=7\times 4\times 3=84$
## Example 2
$$1+3+6+\dots+55=$$
1. 55 is the 10th triangular number, so n=10 and the answer is $\frac{10(10+1)(10+2)}6=10\times 11\times 2=220$
